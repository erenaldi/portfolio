About

Building things that fly.

I'm an engineering student with a passion for aerospace systems and precision mechanical design. This portfolio represents a complete 54mm high-power rocket system I designed from scratch in Onshape — every component modeled, constrained, and exported as production-ready STEP files.

The project spans the full scope of rocket engineering: structural airframe couplers, aerodynamic fin geometry, a custom avionics bay housing a flight computer and altimeter, a GPS tracker integration mount, and a precision fabrication jig for consistent fin alignment.

Custom 54mm HPR Rocket System

A complete set of components designed from scratch in Onshape for a 54mm high-power rocket. All models are exported as industry-standard STEP AP242 files and available in the repository.

Avionics

54mm Rocket Electronics Bay

A fully assembled avionics bay for the 54mm rocket — the most complex component in the system. Designed entirely in Onshape as a multi-part assembly, it features a central sled mounted between two circular bulkheads on threaded standoffs. The sled houses a flight computer and barometric altimeter for deployment charge triggering and flight data logging, with a rectangular access cutout and vent port.

Multi-part assembly 54mm airframe fit Dual bulkhead design

Structural

54mm HPR Coupler

A two-stage airframe coupler designed to join two sections of the 54mm rocket body. The upper cylinder (50mm bore) inserts into one tube while the conical lower collar seats flush against the other, providing structural continuity and axial load transfer across the joint. Retention screw holes ensure positive locking.

50mm bore Conical transition Retention hardware

Aerodynamics

54mm HPR Swept Fin

A swept fin for the 54mm rocket with a triangular planform and beveled leading edge to minimize drag at transonic speeds. The rectangular root tab (12mm) slots precisely into the airframe fin slots for secure, repeatable attachment — designed for fiberglass or G10 construction.

GPS / Tracking

BT-60 Jiobit GPS Holder

A BT-60 coupler (39mm bore) with an integrated interior cradle sized to seat a Jiobit GPS tracker flush inside the airframe. Designed for zero external protrusion — the tracker is fully enclosed and protected during flight, enabling real-time location tracking and reliable post-flight recovery.

Fabrication Tooling

BT-60 4x Fin Cutting Jig

A precision fabrication jig (45mm OD) that slides over a BT-60 body tube to guide fin slot cuts at exactly 90° intervals. Four evenly spaced vertical slots position a blade at perfect radial symmetry, eliminating layout error during airframe assembly and ensuring consistent fin cant across all four fins.
