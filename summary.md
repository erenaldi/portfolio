# Portfolio Summary

A collection of 3D CAD models for high-power rocketry (HPR) components and related tooling, designed for 54mm and BT-60 airframe standards.

---

## 54mm HPR Coupler
**Files:** `54mm-HPR-Coupler-1.png` / `54mm-HPR-Coupler-1.step`

A two-stage coupler designed for 54mm high-power rocket airframes. The upper section is a straight cylindrical tube that inserts into one body tube, while the lower section tapers outward into a conical collar that seats against the other. Two small alignment or screw holes are visible on the upper cylinder. This part joins two airframe sections together and provides structural continuity across the joint.

---

## 54mm HPR Fins
**Files:** `54mm-HPR-Fins-1.png` / `54mm-HPR-Fins-1.step`

A single swept fin designed for a 54mm high-power rocket. The fin features a triangular swept planform with a beveled leading edge for reduced drag, and a rectangular root tab that slots into the airframe tube for secure attachment. The profile is thin and flat, typical of fiberglass or plywood fins used in high-power rocketry.

---

## 54mm Rocket Electronics Bay
**Files:** `54mm-Rocket-Electronics-Bay-1.png` / `54mm-Rocket-Electronics-Bay-Assembly-1.step`

A fully assembled electronics bay (e-bay) for a 54mm rocket. The assembly consists of a cylindrical sled mounted between two circular bulkheads, with two threaded standoffs running through the center for structural support. The sled houses a flight computer/altimeter (visible green terminal block and black circular component, likely a barometric vent), with a rectangular cutout for component access. This e-bay is used for deployment charge triggering and flight data logging.

---

## BT-60 Coupler Jiobit Holder
**Files:** `BT-60-Coupler-Jiobit-Holder-1.png` / `BT-60-Coupler-Jiobit-Holder-1.step`

A short cylindrical coupler sized for BT-60 body tubes (approx. 1.637" OD) with an integrated interior shelf or cradle. The internal divider is designed to hold a Jiobit GPS tracker, allowing the tracker to be embedded inside the rocket airframe for real-time location tracking during and after flight. A small screw hole on the outer wall provides retention.

---

## BT-60 Tube 4x Fin Cutting Jig
**Files:** `BT-60-Tube-4X-Fin-Cutting-Jig-1.png` / `BT-60-Tube-4X-Fin-Cutting-Jig-1.step`

A cylindrical jig designed to fit over a BT-60 body tube to guide precise fin slot cuts. Four evenly spaced vertical slots are cut into the jig wall, positioning a saw or knife blade at 90° intervals around the tube for accurate 4-fin layouts. A small retention screw hole is present at the base. This is a fabrication tool used during airframe assembly to ensure consistent fin alignment.
